The United States of America, being America, does not agree with rest of the world on uniform unemployment rates. So, it has its own unemployment rates.

  • Bureau of Labor Statistics:

    U1: People who have been unemployed for 15 weeks or more. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed (for 15+ Weeks)}}{\textnormal{Labor Force}} \times 100\)
    3.3%
  • U2: People who are unemployed, this includes temporary and seasonal jobs. \(\frac{\textnormal{Job Losers}}{\textnormal{Labor Force}} \times 100\)
    3.6%
  • U3: People who are unemployed and are actively searching for jobs. Most common figure of unemployment. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed}}{\textnormal{Labor Force}} \times 100\)
    5.9%
  • U4: People who are unemployed and discouraged. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed + Discouraged Workers}}{\textnormal{Labor Force + Discouraged Workers}} \times 100\)
    6.2%
  • U5: People who are unemployed and marginally attached. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed + Marginally Attached}}{\textnormal{Labor Force + Marginally Attached}} \times 100\)
    6.9%
  • U6: Everyone who is not included in the U3 rate. Such as discouraged and underemployed workers. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed + Marginally Attached + Part Time}}{\textnormal{Labor Force + Marginally Attached}} \times 100 \)
    9.8%

    • International Labour Organization

    • LU1: People who have been unemployed for 15 weeks or more. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed (for 15+ Weeks)}}{\textnormal{Labor Force}} \times 100\)

The ONS and other sources gives useful numbers that we can use to calculate the U rates of unemployment.

  • U1: People who have been unemployed for 15 weeks or more. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed (for 15+ Weeks)}}{\textnormal{Labor Force}} \times 100\)
    ~3.1%
  • U2: People who are unemployed, this includes temporary and seasonal jobs. \(\frac{\textnormal{Job Losers}}{\textnormal{Labor Force}} \times 100\)
    N/A
  • U3: People who are unemployed and are actively searching for jobs. Most common figure of unemployment. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed}}{\textnormal{Labor Force}} \times 100\)
    4.8%
  • U4: People who are unemployed and discouraged. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed + Discouraged Workers}}{\textnormal{Labor Force + Discouraged Workers}} \times 100\)
    ~16.6%
  • U5: People who are unemployed and marginally attached. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed + Marginally Attached}}{\textnormal{Labor Force + Marginally Attached}} \times 100\)
    N/A
  • U6: Everyone who is not included in the U3 rate. Such as discouraged and underemployed workers. \(\frac{\textnormal{Unemployed + Marginally Attached + Part Time}}{\textnormal{Labor Force + Marginally Attached}} \times 100 \)
    N/A

Sources:

  1. United Kingdom - Labor Force
    2.
  2. LFS: Econ. inactivity reasons: Discouraged workers: UK: 16-64:%
  3. LFS: Economically Inactive: UK: All: Aged 16-64: Thousands: SA
  4. Labour market overview, UK: July 2021
    5.

Calculations:

  • U1: According to Source 1, the labour force of the U.K. is 53,557,000 and the number of unemployed people is 1,637,000. Thus, \(\frac{1,637,000}{53,557,000} \times 100 = 3.1 \%\). The reason why there is a tilda sign is because I am unsure if this number is for 15+ weeks and over.
  • U2: Unfourtunately, I was unable to find job losers or a statistic similar to that for the U.K.
  • U3: This is given by the Office of Labour Statistics.
  • U4: According to Source 2, during March-May 2021, 0.8% of all 16-64 year olds who were economically inactive said that they were discouraged in the Labour Force Survey. According to Source 3, there were 8,806,000 economicaly inactive people during March-May 2021. Thus, \(\frac{8,806,000 + 70448 }{53,557,000 + 70448 } \times 100 = 16.6 \%\). The reason why there is a tilda sign is because I am using economically inactive, not unemployed.
  • U5: While I was able to find those who were marginally attached, the data is two years old. Thus, I am unable to provide an accurate mesaurement of the U5 rate for the U.K.
  • U6: Due to the missing variable of those who are marginally attatched, I am unable to calculate the U6 rate.
